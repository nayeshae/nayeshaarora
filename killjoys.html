01. Overview

This zine project explores the uneasy blur between human creativity and machine-made images. It documents my journey navigating the tension of authorship and responsibility while working with generative AI tools, serving as a critical reflection on what machines truly "carry" when they create.

02. The Process

Grappling with Authorship

To explore the uneasy blur between human creativity and machine-made images, I spoke with artists about responsibility and the strange emotions tied to working with AI. The zine fragments these conversations, layering textures and theory to document the tension of working with generative systems.

Methodology

I utilized a process of trial, error, and duality. By juxtaposing AI output against human critique, I highlighted the labor that speed usually hides.

Key Themes

Authorship, algorithmic haunting, and the tension between perceived speed and actual creative craft.

04. Visual Identity

Stripped-Down & Visceral

To capture the tension between human craft and machine output, I abandoned decorative design in favor of a stark, high-friction visual language. The aesthetic logic is defined by friction, duality, and sensory residue:

  • Restricted Palette: Aggressive red, void black, and stark off-white create a warning-like atmosphere echoing algorithmic haunting.
  • Bold Typography: Oversized black headlines juxtaposed against fragmented text mirror the cognitive dissonance discussed by artists.
  • Analog/Digital Blur: Hand-drawn scribbles mixed with AI output physically anchor the invisible human labor that machines erase.

Sequential Palette

A somatic scale where darker reds represent algorithmic intensity and cognitive void, while signature reds anchor human friction and emotional residue. The stark off-white provides a necessary grounding contrast, preventing visual fatigue across the dense spreads.

05. Closing

Journey Ends

This project was a deep dive into the emotional landscape of generative AI, pushing me to confront the tension between speed and authorship. Here are some standout reflections.

Winning Moments

  • Capturing the Friction: Successfully translating complex, abstract artist interviews into a visceral, tangible editorial format.
  • Embracing Imperfection: Learning to let go of "smooth" tech-focused design to let the raw, messy nature of the subject matter dictate the visual language.
  • Provoking Discomfort: Creating a piece that doesn't just display information, but physically forces the reader to sit with the unease of machine-made art.

Lessons Learned

  • The Medium is the Message: Realizing that the physical constraints and stark aesthetic choices of a zine communicate just as much as the written content itself.
  • Navigating Hypocrisy: Grappling with the ethical gray areas of using the exact tools (generative AI) I was critiquing, and finding a way to make that contradiction visible rather than hiding it.
  • Pacing the Reader: Understanding that "speed hides the labor," and deliberately using dense layouts and harsh contrast to slow down the reading experience.
